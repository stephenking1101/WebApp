Task: Design Project Deliverables

Purpose

The Design process is performed in order to define how the project deliverables will be constructed during the Build activity and delivered to the customer during Implementation. Because the actual Design process is so closely coupled to the technical domain the actual work of design may be governed by methodologies external to RBPM. The core processes listed here are generic by nature and the technical team charged with completing the design should follow the methodology specific to the project's technical domain. The primary RBPM processes in this activity are limited to project management activities of monitoring and reporting progress of the technical teams.

Background

Design is the technical activity that transforms the business requirements into a definition of what will be implemented and how it will be constructed.

The Design activity is initiated once the business requirements are approved by the Project Sponsor/Budget Owner, but is revisited following the detection of a defect resulting from flawed design or a change in the requirements or build specifications.

Steps

Manage Design

Management of the processes that comprise the Design activity is essential to ensure that all design deliverables, appropriate to the project technical domain, are sufficiently detail to facilitate succeeding processes and are delivered within the constraints of time, cost and quality. This is an ongoing process that continues throughout the entire design activity.

At the start of Design, the project manager should establish a log of artefacts to be delivered by the technical team(s). The log should include the following:

  • Artefact name
  • Technical team responsible for it
  • Team lead with contact information
  • Expected delivery date
  • Current status and status date
  • Comments on delivery of the artefact

The project manager should regularly update the log via scheduled artefact status from the technical team lead(s) for reporting purposes. The frequency and format of the status gathering may be documented in the overall project communication plan.

Deliverables

A complete set of design deliverables is reviewed and approved according to the dictates of the technical domain methodology.

Review Design

The deliverables are reviewed and approved in meetings between the design team(s) and build team(s). Other key stakeholder representatives are included as is deemed appropriate by the project manager.

Guidance

Deliverables may be reviewed and approved individually as they are completed. They may be reviewed in groups as appropriate to the project methodology or development structure (for example, all functional design deliverables may be reviewed as a group).

Review and approval of the design deliverables is generally an iterative process with the technical teams receiving feedback, incorporating it into the deliverables and re-submitting them for review and approval until a consensus on approval is reached.

Certfiy Build Readiness

The project manager holds a final build readiness workshop with key stakeholders from the design technical teams, user representatives and build teams to validate the readiness of all design deliverables to instruct the Build team in how to successfully construct the project product/system/service.

Guidance

The workshop produces a build readiness certificate that all appropriate stakeholders sign-off. This document is typically determined by the project technical domain and it certifies that the project is ready to start the build activity. The project manager may use an e-mail requesting approval by all key design stakeholders as the certificate. The approved document should be filed in the project document repository.
