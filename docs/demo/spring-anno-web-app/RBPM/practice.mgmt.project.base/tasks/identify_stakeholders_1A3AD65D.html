Task: Identify Stakeholders

Identify the stakeholders of the project and their specific areas of interest.

Purpose

In the course of a project there are many individuals and groups who have an interest in it, are involved in it, or are affected by its activities and outcomes. These parties are the stakeholders. A stakeholder matrix is useful in identifying and planning communications, using the most appropriate communication mechanism for each group of stakeholders. Where appropriate, stakeholders can be segmented into logical groups with the most suitable communications channel being used for each group.

Procedure

  1. Create a stakeholder matrix listing each of the stakeholders by their particular interest in the programme or project. Identify whether the stakeholder is active in the programme or project, interested in it, an authorisation party etc.

    If necessary, further analysis on the stakeholders can be done where it will help to make communications to and from these stakeholders more effective.

  2. For each major stakeholder or stakeholder group, record the following information:

    Attitude to change – are they for or against the programme or project.

    Sphere of influence – and an indication of scale of influence.

    Level of contribution – is the stakeholder providing any form of resource to the programme or project

    Tolerance to risk – the stakeholder's willingness to take risks.

    Expectations – the stakeholder's expected outcomes for the programme or project in the areas of time, cost, quality and scope.

Key Considerations

Some stakeholders may change during the life of a project. The stakeholder matrix must be regularly maintained by the project manager throughout the life of the project.
