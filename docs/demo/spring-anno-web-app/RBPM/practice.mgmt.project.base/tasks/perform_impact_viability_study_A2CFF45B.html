Task: Perform Impact Analysis and Viability Study

Purpose

To assess the need, requirements and scope for the subsequent Viability Study.
It examines the selected solution, as documented in the Idea Outline and the Investment Summary, and performs an analysis of the likely impacts in terms of the architectural, business and technological complexity. At each of these stages, the aim is to clarify any major variances between the selected solution, and what is known, understood, or proven within the Group, or is at variance with well-proven industry experience.

Steps

Conduct Impact Analysis

The Impact Analysis activity is performed in order to assess the need, requirements and scope for the subsequent Viability Study.

It examines the selected solution, as documented in the Idea Outline and the Investment Summary, and performs an analysis of the likely impacts in terms of the architectural, business and technological complexity. At each of these stages, the aim is to clarify any major variances between the selected solution, and what is known, understood, or proven within the Group, or is at variance with well-proven industry experience.

A formal Impact Analysis document is produced and reviewed. This recommends whether the subsequent Viability Study should:

  • be carried out at all
  • be reduced in scope or content
  • include additional elements which have been identified as high risk.

Background

Impact Analysis is carried out by the Project Manager after the Investment Summary for the project have been defined. This activity builds and expands on the risk assessments already earlier in the project lifecycle, in order to determine the scope and level of detail required in a possible subsequent viability study document.

Where low levels of risk are identified in certain areas, then the need to carry out the full Viability Study is reduced, and effort is therefore likely to be reduced in subsequent activities. In some cases, the recommendation may be that no Viability Study is performed, and instead the project moves directly to the Requirements Gathering process.

Perform Viability Study

The Viability Study activity is performed in order to establish the business, technical viability, and/or the architectural compliance of the solution selected the Prioritisation process. The study is carried out according to the scope recommended in the Impact Analysis Report.

The Viability Study Report recommends whether the organisation should proceed with the project or, if necessary, to revisit the Clear Choice document or the clear choice selection in Prioritisation.

Background

A Viability Study is carried out by the Project Manager, based on the findings of the Impact Analysis Report. This process determines the practicality (that is, viability) of the project in different areas including business, technical, and architectural compliance viability.

The aim of Viability is to investigate areas of uncertainty, identified in Impact Analysis, until they are no longer uncertain and their impact is understood.
