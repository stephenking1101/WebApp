Task: Build Project Deliverables

Purpose

The Build process is performed in order to create the deliverables needed to fulfil the Business Requirements and verify that they are ready for use within the following project activity. The following activity may vary by project technical domain (e.g., Testing for Software Engineering projects, Occupation for Building Construction projects, etc.).

Background

Build is the construction activity that transforms the Design deliverables into the final project deliverables that provide the functionality requested by the Project Sponsor/Budget Owner. The Build deliverables are tightly coupled with the project technical domain and may include new business process, software modules, a new physical site, training coursework, marketing campaign, operational procedures, etc

The Build activity is initiated following the certification of the Build readiness of the Design deliverables. It may be revisited following the detection of a defect resulting from flawed Build tasks, design flaws, changes in the design deliverables, or approved changes in requirements.

Scope

Build has to do with the construction and quality control of the deliverables that provide the required functions to the customer. The construction techniques for Build deliverables vary greatly and are tightly coupled to the project technical domain.

The deliverables that will be implemented/installed for the customer's use are created, reviewed, and certified as meeting the customer requirements in this activity.

Steps

Plan and manage build activity

Management of the Build activity is performed to ensure that all project deliverables are delivered within the triple constraints of time, cost and quality and provide the functionality as defined in the Business Requirements.

Plan

The Project Manager works with the Build team(s) to identify all discrete deliverables to be constructed and agrees to frequency of status updates by the team(s). The Project Manager loads their details into the Project Deliverables Log that was created in the Design activity. To ensure traceability of requirements, the Build Deliverables should be related to one or more Design Deliverables. Likewise a single Design Deliverable may relate to more than one Build Deliverable. In other words, there is a many to many relationship between Design and Build deliverables.

The Project Manager updates the project work plan with new detail that arises from these regular Build team status updates.

Manage

Throughout the Build activity, the Project Manager receives regular progress status from the Build team(s). The status is used to update the Project Deliverables Log and for regular progress reporting.

The Project Manager is expected to apply the standard Project Management best practices of managing risk, providing regular status to key stakeholders and manage the project scope, schedule, cost and quality throughout the Build activity.

Review and approve build deliverables

The final core process of the Build activity ensures that all deliverables, appropriate to the project technical domain, deliver all the functionality defined in the Business Requirements and achieve the level of quality defined in the project Quality plan.

Approach

Review and Approve Build Deliverables consists of two stages, Build Deliverables Review and Build Deliverables Certification. The Build Deliverable Review is conducted by a team of project stakeholders with the requisite technical skills in the technical domain to validate that the deliverables are of sufficient quality and that they deliver the functionality to the customer as defined in the Business Requirements. It may be accomplished on all deliverables at once, on individual deliverables as they are completed or in phases or groups of deliverables as appropriate to the project plan.

Build Deliverables Certification is a final review of all approved Build Deliverables that are to be implemented in the current project phase. It is accomplished in a meeting chaired by the Project Manager and attended by key stakeholders representing customers, design team(s), build team(s) and implementation/installation team(s). Other stakeholder groups (e.g., HOST Security, Compliance, Legal, Regulatory agencies, etc.) may participate based on their understanding of the project deliverables and how they are to be used within the customer organization or by customers individually.

Review

The deliverables are reviewed and approved in meetings with the Build team(s) and other key stakeholder representatives as deemed appropriate by the Project Manager. The review process may be iterative with feedback from the reviewers compiled by the Build team and incorporated into the deliverables prior to the next review. Once the reviewers are satisfied with the deliverables, they approve them as ready for Build certification.

Review Guidelines

Deliverables may be reviewed and approved individually as they are completed. They may be reviewed in groups as appropriate to the project methodology or implementation plan such as reviewing all phase 1 deliverables as a group.

Certify build deliverables

The project manager holds a final build certification workshop with key stakeholders from the build team(s), design team(s), user representatives, implementation/installation team(s) and any representatives of any other organisation that may have an interest in the successful completion of the project. The goal of the workshop is to validate the readiness of all design deliverables as of sufficient quality and functionality as defined in earlier activities and that they are ready for the next project activity.

Any deliverable flaws or exceptions to defined quality and functionality specifications that are discovered must be fully documented with mitigation/remediation plans or designated exceptions and approved by the participants as acceptable for proceeding to the next activity. Failure to get such approval will require the build team(s) to correct the deficiencies that must be validated in a follow on build certification workshop.

Certify Guidelines

The workshop produces final Build Readiness certification documentation on which all appropriate Stakeholders sign-off. This documentation certifies that the Build deliverables are of sufficient quality and scope to start the next project activity. The exact form and structure of the documentation may vary according to the project technical domain. It may even take the form of an e-mail summary of the Build Certification workshop recommendations that is circulated to key Stakeholders for approval. All Build Certification documentation must be kept in the project documentation repository for the length of time depending on the legal and compliance dictates of the project environment. For projects with a Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) impact, the retention period for project documentation is seven (7) years.

The next project activity may vary depending on the project technical domain. For example, IT projects will proceed to System or System Integration Test. Construction projects may proceed to building turnover and Business Process Re-engineering projects may proceed to User Training.
