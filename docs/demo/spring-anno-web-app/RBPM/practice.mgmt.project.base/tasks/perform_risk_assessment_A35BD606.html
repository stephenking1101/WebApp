Task: Perform Risk Assessment

To perform the risk assessment:

  1. Different aspects of the risk assessment will require a view from different parties in the project, and you should make sure that these parties are involved. This is likely to include the Business Representative and specific Subject Experts, but this is not an exhaustive list.
  2. For each risk area, consider the implications of the question in the Risk Register. Assess whether there are any specific possibilities that would impact your project and so need to be monitored and mitigated as a risk. As a guide, these should be potential occurrences that would have a tangible impact on the schedule, cost or quality of the project if they occur.
  3. Where a risk has been identified, record it on the risk list as defined in the process. Record the Risk ID in Clarity. This ensures that either the Project Manager or an independent reviewer can identify which particular assessed risk areas have resulted in a managed risk within the risk list.

It is important to identify any risks to sustainability goals early in the project lifecycle. Refer to Considerations for assessing impact for identifying any risks which could impact (both positive and negative) the sustainability goals. Engage a Sustainability Expert, if necessary, to apply appropriate risk mitigation. See, Contacts for Sustainability.
