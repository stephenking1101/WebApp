Task: Plan Quality

Purpose

Quality Planning should begin in the early phases of a project to establish plans, processes, standards, and procedures that will add value and satisfy the requirements of the project and the organisational policies. It is the process of identifying the applicable processes, standards and procedures and determining steps to satisfy them. It should be performed in parallel with project planning. The Project Manager should ensure that all the stakeholders are aware of the Quality Management policy.
  1. Based on the project scope, identify the specific project quality requirements and deliverables for reviews. The applicable processes, standards, and procedures for the project are also identified.
  2. The Quality Plan includes the Quality Management activities for the project. This also includes the responsibilities and appropriate schedule along with the dependencies on the related project tasks planned. The Quality Plan is part of the Project Plan
  3. Ensure that reference documents and deliverables are available to perform the reviews.
  4. Ensure that the applicable standards and checklists, which are used to perform a given set of activities or tasks, or to verify that a given set of activities or tasks has been performed, are available to the staff and quality staff.
