Task: Initiate Engagements

Purpose

Initiate formal engagements with all the teams upon which the project has dependencies.

Steps

Engage Procurement

Procurement must be contacted if the project entails money being spent outside of exampleCom.

Procedure

  1. In consultation with the Procurement and Subject Experts, establish the extent to which the required functionality differentiates exampleCom from the market.
    • Where the required functionality is unique to exampleCom and, therefore, offers competitive advantage, consideration should be given to developing in-house.
    • Where the required functionality is common within the market and merely provides parity with competitors, consideration should be given to contracting a supplier to provide the goods or services.
  2. Refer to the Group Systems List to establish if there is an existing suitable solution in use within the Group. Reuse of existing solutions is highly recommended to allow the Group to gain best value from previous investments.
  3. Check existing contract conditions for existing suppliers with Procurement – a favourable existing contract may encourage exampleCom to buy if the conditions make building an inefficient use of internal resources.
  4. Check with Management, Procurement and HOST Planning, Finance, and Quality to establish if there are similar developments or on-going negotiations for similar goods or services.
  5. Based on these factors, decide whether it is appropriate to engage a supplier.

Guidelines

The Group approach is to build for competitive advantage, buy for parity.

Engage BRCM

All projects must ensure that they adequately identify, assess, monitor and control operational risk by adopting the Group’s Operational Risk and Internal Control (ORIC) Management Framework.

Procedure

At a minimum, if the proposed project involves a Financial Accounting/Financial Reporting process (SOX); or a system/process that affects a customer facing service, then please contact your BRCM team to review relevant Risk and Control Assessment (RCA) and evaluate potential impacts to the control environment that could be caused by the project. The RCA process is designed to provide businesses with a forward looking view of operational risk and to help them proactively determine whether their key operational risks are controlled within acceptable levels.

If the project does not involve a SOX process or a customer facing service, please ensure you review the D.1.1 Risk Prioritisation Matrix in the Operations FIM. If after reviewing the matrix, you assess that the proposed project introduces anything greater than low risk; your project requires BRCM involvement. Please contact your BRCM team for additional support.

If BRCM involvement is required, then the potential adverse impacts to the control environment must be logged as a project risk in your risk register, so it can be monitored throughout the project lifecycle.

Engage Information Security and Risk

Assess the business owner's tolerance for, and potential impact of, threats that may compromise the availability, integrity or confidentiality of project deliverables.

Procedure

Engage Information Security Risk team to define the ISR requirements based on the overall business (eg. System functionalities, system migration / conversion), regulatory, and compliance requirements and the impact of potential security breaches.

Engage Sustainability Lead

If your project is going to contribute to the organisation's sustainability goals, engage a Sustainability Expert to help you deliver the sustainability savings.

Refer to Contacts for Sustainability to find the right expert based on your project contribution to the sustainability goals.
