Milestone: Definition Tollgate

Check if the project is on the right track to deliver the agreed benefits / goals

Make the decision on whether to reject the project, loop to redo elements of defintion (agreed at the meeting), or proceed to next stage

Requirements
  • Detailed requirements have been gathered where required for different aspects of the solution.
  • Stakeholders from all impacted business areas and countries have been consulted on detailed requirements.
  • Detailed requirements support high level business requirements, with clear tiering and traceability.
  • For global changes, localisation requirements for individual country or business area needs have been identified and considered during scoping and prioritisation.
  • Detailed requirements have been scoped and prioritised.  Detailed requirements remain relevant to the overall change vision/objectives and there is no scope creep.
  • Detailed Requirements artefacts have been reviewed, agreed and signed off with the appropriate design authorities and at a summary level with the Sponsor and Steering Committee.
  • Detailed requirements are supported with additional context where required (Design Considerations, Business Rules, Data Model Impacts)
Project Controls
  • Review project controls and plan necessary actions as agreed for applicable project controls (e.g. Operational Risk, Records Management, SOX etc.)
  • All project documents and artefacts are stored at an agreed central repository (e.g. SharePoint) with appropriate access control. Key documents and artefacts are stored appropriately, timely; managed under change control once signed off and are correctly classified.
Customer Centered Decisioning
  • Customer-related changes have been scoped and designed where needed.  All new solutions enhance the customer experience. Customer Value Management (CVM), Distribution and Propositions teams have been involved as appropriate.
  • All customer and customer facing team impacts have been fully  considered, agreed and signed off by all impacted areas.
Plan, Budget, Resource, Risks and Issues
  • Detailed planning has been conducted.  Project manager and delivery partners have been involved in planning.
  • There is a plan for project documented.  Milestones have been identified within the plan.  The plan has been baselined.
  • Dependencies have been identified within the plan and to other programmes and projects.   The critical path has been identified in the plan.
  • Execution stage tollgates have been agreed, included in the plan and criteria defined.
  • Project closure criteria and outcomes required have been defined and agreed with the business.
  • Detailed effort estimates and resource requirements have been completed.  Roles have been set up on Clarity.
  • Contracts with suppliers and engagements with delivery partners have been agreed and initiated.
  • Project costs are known.  The budget has been defined and baselined.
  • Project risks and issues have been identified and documented with mitigating actions. Ownership for risks and issues is clear and mitigating actions have been kicked off.
  • Sustainability impacts have been assessed and reviewed.
  • The relevant quality controls to meet bankâ€™s quality assurance guidelines/standards have been included in plan.
  • Reporting to support project governance has been agreed, including format and frequency.
  • Project document repository has been set up.  Key project documents and artefacts are being managed under change control once signed off.
  • The risks, budget and plan, especially the critical path and milestones have been reviewed, agreed and signed off with the Project Sponsor and Steering Committee.
Investment Summary
  • Cost and benefit projections have been updated to reflect revisions during Definition.
  • The cost projections remain within Annual Operating Plan (AOP) and affordability constraints.
  • Cost, benefit and timeline estimates have been tightened to reflect increased certainty and a +/-10% contingency level (norm - can be higher or lower dependent on the type of change).
  • A full benefits plan is in place with benefits measures/KPIs and targets at key milestones per benefit owner. The benefits desired at any point in time are clear and are realistic based on the roadmap.
  • Benefits realisation activities are reflected in the Change & Implementation and other workstream plans.
  • The full Investment Summary (and Investment Framework if applicable) has been iterated to a full version.
  • The full Investment Summary (and Investment Framework if applicable) has been reviewed, agreed and signed-off with the Project Sponsor and Steering Committee.
  • Does the proposed project involve a Financial Accounting or Financial Reporting process within a business unit that may impact the Annual Report?
  • If SOX needs to be considered, have the Project Governance Procedures for the Sarbanes Oxley Act (2002) been followed?
Readiness for Execution Stage
  • There is funding available for the Execution stage.
  • Change Control Process has been established and initiated
  • There is a clear plan for the Execution stage. Responsibilities and milestone dates have been communicated.
  • Resource requirements for Execution have been raised and all resources/teams are being mobilised. There is standard project onboarding material in place for project team members and key stakeholders.
  • Delivery partner responsibilities during Execution have been agreed.  Where needed, external support has been sourced and contracts have been signed.
  • There is adequate sponsor, business stakeholder and business SME access for Execution stage. An effective Steering Committee is in place, with empowered members and effective decision making.
  • Execution stage tollgates have been agreed, included in the plan and criteria defined. These may include tollgates at the end of delivery phases, around deployments or implementation phases.
  • Relevant quality controls to meet bank's quality assurance guidelines/standards been agreed
  • At least one Project Review was conducted during Definition.  Further reviews are scheduled throughout Execution and Change, Transition & Closure
  • Does the proposed project involve a Financial Accounting or Financial Reporting process within a business unit that may impact the Annual Report?
  • If SOX needs to be considered, have the Project Governance Procedures for the Sarbanes Oxley Act (2002) been followed?
