Milestone: Design Tollgate

Check if the end to end design of project deliverables is on the right track to deliver the agreed benefits / goals.

Make the decision on whether to reject the project, loop to redo elements of pre-implementation (agreed at the meeting), or proceed to next stage.

Design
  • Detailed designs have been created and documented with the right delivery partner involvement (eg HOST and global functions) where needed.
  • Detailed designs have been created and documented with the right delivery partner involvement (eg HOST and global functions) where needed.
  • People and Organisation changes have been scoped and designed where needed.  HR teams are involved.
  • Are the impacts on customers and customer facing processes understood?
  • Customer-related changes have been scoped and designed where needed.  CVM, Distribution and Propositions teams have been involved as appropriate.
  • Channels-related changes have been scoped and designed where needed.  Distribution teams have been involved.
  • Product and proposition related changes have been scoped and designed where needed.  Propositions and Risk teams have been involved as appropriate.
  • Operations changes (e.g. process, procedures, service management, operations management etc.) have been scoped and designed where needed.  Service Delivery teams and business process owners have been involved. 
  • If Project Controls are identified, are necessary actions taken and required approvals in place?
  • Support Channels changes have been scoped and designed where needed.
  • Supplier changes have been scoped and designed where needed.  Vendors have been contracted if it is a system or procured service change (with ITO/SWD/SD/Procurement).  Procurement and the business area receiving the services have been involved for changes relating to external suppliers.
  • If Facilities changes are required they have been scoped and designed.  CRE teams have been involved.
  • If System and infrastructure changes are required they have been scoped and designed.  SWD and ITO have been involved.
  • If Data changes are required they  have been scoped and designed.  SWD and ITO have been involved.  Information security and data management teams have been involved.
  • Governance changes have been scoped and designed where needed.
  • Management Information changes have been scoped and designed where needed.
  • Any must have dispensations from Group standards have been identified and agreed with the responsible function or business area e.g. architecture/security/FIM etc.
  • For global changes, the areas for localisation to individual country or business area needs of the global solution have been considered in the design. Any exceptions or dispensations for specific countries or business areas have been identfied and agreed by the business sponsor and steering committee.
  • Detailed design support delivery of the Business Requirements, the Solution Design and detailed requirements with full traceability. Detailed scope has been defined and this reflects high level scope. The detailed designs remain relevant to the change vision/objectives and there is no scope creep.
  • Detailed designs will work effectively together.  Effort has been made to create alignment and integration between different detailed designs.
  • Detailed designs consider integration with existing business designs and the business current state (e.g. other processes, systems etc.).
  • Detailed design artefacts have been reviewed, agreed and signed off with the appropriate design authorities and at a summary level with the Sponsor and Steering Committee.
Approach to Change & Implementation
  • All stakeholders have been engaged.  Stakeholder goals and expectations are understood. Stakeholders are engaged in taking actions to make the change happen.
  • A detailed communications plan is in place and early communications activity has commenced.
  • If applicable, detailed training requirements and designs have been completed.
  • If applicable, detailed requirements and designs for organisation alignment and operations alignment have been completed (e.g. process changes, job description changes etc.).
